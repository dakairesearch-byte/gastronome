# the-diner
**Lens:** Urban 30-50, dines out 2-4x/week, opens this on a Tuesday at 6pm asking "where do I go tonight?"
**Reviewed:** screenshots (home-desktop, home-mobile, restaurant-desktop, search-desktop) + `src/app/page.tsx`, `src/components/Navigation.tsx`, `src/components/RestaurantCard.tsx`.

## Top 5 findings

1. **[P0] What's wrong:** Home has no search bar, no city switcher, no "open now / near me / tonight" entry point — just "Suggestions" pinned to New York (`page.tsx:14, 62-66`) with cards that hide price, distance, and hours (`home-desktop.png` cards show only name + cuisine pill + star + count). **Why it matters:** I'm in Brooklyn at 6pm. I can't ask the homepage *anything*. Yelp's homepage takes a query and a location in one move; Google Maps shows what's open within 10 min. Here I'd bounce to either within 5 seconds. **What to do:** Add a hero search ("Find dinner in ___, tonight, $$") above Suggestions; surface my city from `profiles.home_city`; default the suggestion grid to "open now within 1mi" when geolocation is granted. **Why you'd want to do this:** Turns the home from a magazine cover into a destination decision tool — the only reason I'd use Gastronome over Google. (effort: M)

2. **[P0] What's wrong:** No price ($/$$/$$$/$$$$), no hours, no distance, no reservation availability on the compact home card (`RestaurantCard.tsx:96-131`). Hero variant has price (line 265-272), but the home/suggestions cards do not. **Why it matters:** Price is the #1 filter for a Tuesday-night decision. OpenTable shows price + available times in one glance; here I have to click into each card to learn if it's $40 or $400. After three clicks I've quit. **What to do:** Add `$$` next to cuisine pill in compact variant; render "Open until 10pm" or "Closed" from Google Places hours; add a small reservation-availability hint if we have OpenTable/Resy data. **Why you'd want to do this:** Cards become decisionable, not just browsable. (effort: S for price; M for hours/reservation)

3. **[P1] What's wrong:** Two of three home sections are empty for new/anonymous users — "Recent Searches" shows "No recent searches yet" and "Your Favorites" shows the same flavor of empty (`home-desktop.png` mid-page, `page.tsx:92-102`). **Why it matters:** A first-time diner sees a half-broken homepage with two blank holes. Trust drops instantly; this looks abandoned. **What to do:** Collapse empty modules until populated, OR replace with "Trending in NYC tonight" and "Neighborhood picks: Williamsburg" using existing trending data. **Why you'd want to do this:** Removes the abandoned-app smell and uses prime real estate for something useful. (effort: S)

4. **[P1] What's wrong:** "Saved Collections" tiles (Date Night, Quick Lunch, Special Occasions, Hidden Gems) are placeholder facades that just link to a generic explore filter (`page.tsx:20-53`). "Date Night" → `?cuisine=French` is reductive (date night is not a cuisine), and "Quick Lunch" → `?cuisine=Sandwich` is borderline absurd for a serious diner. **Why it matters:** This is the most editorial real estate on the page and it reads like a stock-photo demo. If a category that promises "Hidden Gems" returns the same restaurants I see everywhere, I've learned this app doesn't actually have a curatorial point of view. **What to do:** Either build real curated collections (5-10 restaurants picked by a human, dated, with a 1-line "why") or rename to honest filters ("French Bistros", "Quick Bites"). **Why you'd want to do this:** Editorial curation is the only durable moat vs Google — fake curation actively destroys the moat. (effort: M for real collections; S for honest renaming)

5. **[P1] What's wrong:** Restaurant detail page (`restaurant-desktop.png`) leads with a hero photo that's *not* of food (it's a dark interior) and the "Ratings Dashboard" shows 4.3 / 3.7 with two unlabeled bar fragments. A live Google Maps API error panel renders mid-page where the map should be. **Why it matters:** As a serious diner I came here for: what's the food, what does it cost, can I get in tonight. I'm getting an empty room and a stack trace. The aggregator's whole pitch — "Google says X, Yelp says Y, Michelin agrees" — should be the loudest element, not buried. **What to do:** Prioritize a food photo for the hero (fall back to interior only if no dish photos exist); replace the broken map with a clean address + "Get directions" link until the key is restored; make the source comparison the visual centerpiece (logo + score + count side-by-side). **Why you'd want to do this:** This is the page where trust either gets earned or lost. Right now it loses. (effort: M)

## Quick wins (max 5, no severity)

- Add `$$` price chip to the compact `RestaurantCard` (data exists, just not rendered) — `RestaurantCard.tsx:101-128`.
- Replace "Suggestions" header label with something temporal ("Hot in NYC this week" or "Trending tonight") — currently reads like a generic CMS block; we already pull 7-day trending.
- Surface "Open now" badge on cards using Google Places opening_hours (we already enrich these per CLAUDE.md pipeline).
- On home, default the suggestion city to `profiles.home_city` (set during onboarding pane 3) instead of hardcoded NYC for logged-in users — `page.tsx:14`.
- Drop the empty "Recent Searches" / "Your Favorites" panels for unauthenticated visitors and stretch Saved Collections full-width.

## Bigger bets (max 2)

- **Tonight Mode on the homepage.** A single primary CTA — "What's good tonight?" — that asks me 3 questions in a row (neighborhood, party size, budget) and returns 5 restaurants ranked by aggregate score + availability + walking distance. This is the one workflow Yelp and Google can't do well because they're not aggregators. **The tradeoff:** Building a real "tonight" engine needs hours data + reservation integration (Resy/OpenTable) + geolocation handling — meaningful eng investment, and it concentrates the brand on a single use case (deciding to eat *now*), which may underweight the planning/inspiration use case that Explore covers.

- **Editorial "This Week" issue.** Replace generic Saved Collections with a weekly editorial drop — 1 human-curated list of 8-12 restaurants ("Bourdain-approved holdouts still standing", "Where the chefs eat on Mondays") with a paragraph of context. Anchors the brand to taste, not algorithm. **The tradeoff:** Requires an actual writer and weekly cadence; if you skip a week, the homepage looks stale faster than an algorithmic feed would. But this is the only thing that makes me text the link to a friend.

## Alarming

Live Google Maps API error rendered to users mid-restaurant-page (`restaurant-desktop.png`) — exposes infra plumbing and shouts "not ready". Fail silently with an address + directions link.
